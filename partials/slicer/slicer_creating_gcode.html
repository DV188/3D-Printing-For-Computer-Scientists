Creating G-code

Now that you have a better understanding on how Slic3r's print settings work, let's take a look at how Slic3r creates the G-code required by the printer. Add an .stl to Repetier-Host's workspace. Navigate to the slicer panel towards the right of the application and select he Slicer with Slic3r button. For our examples, we will be using the simplest .stl cube created previously.

cube.stl

Repetier-Host will bring you to the G-code screen. G-code generated by Slic3r is laid out as well as some console output for the steps taken to slice your model.

G-code has been around for a long time and is a standard for Cartesian based CNC units. We will delve into how to parse the G-code, but for now, your model is ready to be printed by your printer.
